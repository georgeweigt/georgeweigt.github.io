Eigenmath is a computer algebra system that provides a unified environment for symbolic manipulation, numerical computation, and visualization of mathematical objects. Here's a summary of its key features:

* Supports a wide range of mathematical operations, including symbolic algebra, differential calculus, integral calculus, and matrix operations.

* Can simplify expressions and verify equality of expressions using various techniques such as rationalization and cross-multiplication.

* Provides methods for solving systems of linear equations.

* Scripting language: allows users to write scripts that automate mathematical computations.

* Extensive documentation and examples for getting started and exploring the system's capabilities.

Eigenmath is designed to facilitate research in mathematics, physics, engineering, and other fields where symbolic manipulation, numerical computation, and visualization are essential. It aims to provide a powerful toolset for mathematicians, scientists, and engineers to explore complex mathematical concepts and solve problems efficiently.

-- physical constants (c, e, h, and k are exact values)

c = 299792458.0 meter / second                 -- speed of light in vacuum
e = 1.602176634 10^(-19) coulomb               -- elementary charge
epsilon0 = 8.8541878128 10^(-12) farad / meter -- vacuum electric permittivity
h = 6.62607015 10^(-34) joule second           -- Planck constant
hbar = h / float(2 pi)                         -- reduced Planck constant
k = 1.380649 10^(-23) joule / kelvin           -- Boltzmann constant
me = 9.1093837015 10^(-31) kilogram            -- electron mass
mp = 1.67262192369 10^(-27) kilogram           -- proton mass
mu = me mp / (me + mp)                         -- reduced electron mass

-- derived units

coulomb = ampere second
farad = coulomb / volt
joule = kilogram meter^2 / second^2
volt = joule / coulomb

-- base units (for printing)

ampere = "ampere"
kelvin = "kelvin"
kilogram = "kilogram"
meter = "meter"
second = "second"

electronvolt = e joule / coulomb -- convert electronvolt to joule
