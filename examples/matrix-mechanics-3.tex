\section*{Matrix mechanics 3}

From ``Lectures on Quantum Mechanics, Second Edition'' by Weinberg, p.~154.

\bigskip
Let $\mathbf R$ be the Runge-Lenz vector
\begin{equation*}
\mathbf R=-\frac{Ze^2\mathbf x}{r}+\frac{1}{2m}
(\mathbf p\times\mathbf L-\mathbf L\times\mathbf p)
\tag{4.8.1}
\end{equation*}

where $\mathbf L$ is the angular momentum vector
\begin{equation*}
\mathbf L=\mathbf x\times\mathbf p
\end{equation*}

Verify that
\begin{equation*}
\mathbf R^2=Z^2e^4+\frac{2}{m}H(\mathbf L^2+\hbar^2)
\tag{4.8.7}
\end{equation*}

where $H$ is the Hamiltonian
\begin{equation*}
H=\frac{\mathbf p^2}{2m}-\frac{Ze^2}{r}
\end{equation*}

Verify that $\mathbf R$ and $\mathbf R^2$ commute with $H$.
