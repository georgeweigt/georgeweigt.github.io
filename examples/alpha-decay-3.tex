\section*{Alpha decay 3}

In this section we derive a regression formula from the data set
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
& \log\tau_{1/2} & E
\\[1ex]
{}^{226}\mathrm U & -1.31 & 7.70
\\
{}^{228}\mathrm U & \phantom{-}6.30 & 6.80
\\
{}^{230}\mathrm U & 14.4 & 5.99
\\
{}^{232}\mathrm U & 21.5 & 5.41
\\
{}^{234}\mathrm U & 29.7 & 4.86
\\
{}^{236}\mathrm U & 34.2 & 4.57
\\
{}^{238}\mathrm U & 39.5 & 4.27
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

From the previous sections we have
\begin{equation*}
\log\tau_{1/2}\propto\gamma
\end{equation*}

and
\begin{equation*}
\gamma=C_1\frac{Z}{\sqrt E}-C_2\sqrt{Zr_1}
\end{equation*}

Combine the formulas as
\begin{equation*}
\log\tau_{1/2}=\beta_1\frac{90}{\sqrt E}+\beta_0
\end{equation*}

The nuclear radius $r_1\propto A^{1/3}$ is discarded
because it does not change very much.

\bigskip
By ordinary least squares we obtain
\begin{align*}
\beta_1&=3.682
\\
\beta_0&=-120.8
\end{align*}

Hence
\begin{equation*}
\log\tau_{1/2}=3.682\frac{90}{\sqrt E}-120.8
\end{equation*}

From the regression formula we obtain the following predicted values.
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
& \text{Predicted}
\\
\log\tau_{1/2} & \log\tau_{1/2}
\\[1ex]
-1.31 & -1.40
\\
\phantom{-}6.30 & \phantom{-}6.27
\\
14.4 & 14.6
\\
21.5 & 21.6
\\
29.7 & 29.5
\\
34.2 & 34.1
\\
39.5 & 39.5
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

The observed linearity of $\log\tau_{1/2}$ and $Z/\sqrt E$
confirms the tunneling hypothesis for alpha decay.
