\section*{Alpha decay 3}

In this section we derive a regression formula for the following half-life data.
Rows with $Z=90$ are uranium and $Z=88$ are thorium.
Recall that $Z$ is atomic number minus two.
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
\log\tau_{1/2} & A & Z & E
\\[1ex]
6.30 & 228 & 90 & 6.80
\\
14.4 & 230 & 90 & 5.99
\\
21.5 & 232 & 90 & 5.41
\\
29.7 & 234 & 90 & 4.86
\\
34.2 & 236 & 90 & 4.57
\\
39.5 & 238 & 90 & 4.27
\\
7.52 & 226 & 88 & 6.45
\\
17.9 & 228 & 88 & 5.52
\\
28.5 & 230 & 88 & 4.77
\\
40.6 & 232 & 88 & 4.08
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

From the previous sections we have
\begin{equation*}
\log\tau_{1/2}\propto\gamma
\qquad
\gamma=C_1\frac{Z}{\sqrt E}-C_2\sqrt{Zr_1}
\end{equation*}

Noting that $r_1\propto A^{1/3}$ and
to a good approximation $A\propto Z$ we have
\begin{equation*}
\sqrt{Zr_1}\propto\sqrt{ZZ^{1/3}}=Z^{2/3}
\end{equation*}

Hence the Geiger–Nuttall regression model
\begin{equation*}
\log\tau_{1/2}=\beta_2\frac{Z}{\sqrt E}+\beta_1Z^{2/3}+\beta_0
\end{equation*}

By ordinary least squares the best coefficients for the above data are
\begin{align*}
\beta_2&=3.702
\\
\beta_1&=-3.104
\\
\beta_0&=-59.28
\end{align*}

The regression formula yields the following predicted values.
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
& \text{Predicted}
\\
\log\tau_{1/2} & \log\tau_{1/2}
\\[1ex]
6.30 & 6.15
\\
14.4 & 14.5
\\
21.5 & 21.6
\\
29.7 & 29.6
\\
34.2 & 34.2
\\
39.5 & 39.6
\\
7.52 & 7.56
\\
17.9 & 18.0
\\
28.5 & 28.5
\\
40.6 & 40.6
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

The observed linearity of $\log\tau_{1/2}$ and $Z/\sqrt E$
confirms the tunneling hypothesis for alpha decay.
