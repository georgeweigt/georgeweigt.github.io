\section*{Time-independent Schrodinger equation}

Consider the Schrodinger equation
\begin{equation*}
i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(x,t)=H\Psi(x,t)
\end{equation*}

Let $\Psi(x,t)$ be the wavefunction
\begin{equation*}
\Psi(x,t)=\sum_k\xi_k(t)\psi_k(x)
\end{equation*}

where
\begin{equation*}
\xi_k(t)=\exp\left(-\frac{i}{\hbar}E_kt\right)
\end{equation*}

Then for all $k$
\begin{equation*}
i\hbar\frac{d}{dt}\xi_k(t)\psi_k(x)=E_k\xi_k(t)\psi_k(x)
\end{equation*}

and by the Schrodinger equation we have
\begin{equation*}
E_k\xi_k(t)\psi_k(x)=H\left[\xi_k(t)\psi_k(x)\right]
\end{equation*}

Let $H$ be independent of $t$.
Then by linearity of $H$ the right-hand side can be factored as
\begin{equation*}
E_k\xi_k(t)\psi_k(x)=\xi_k(t)H\psi_k(x)
\end{equation*}

The $\xi_k(t)$ cancel leaving the time-independent Schrodinger equation
\begin{equation*}
E_k\psi_k(x)=H\psi_k(x)
\end{equation*}

By linearity of $H$ we also have
\begin{equation*}
\sum_kE_k\psi_k(x)=H\sum_k\psi_k(x)
\end{equation*}
