\section*{Anticommutator example}

Show that
\begin{equation*}
\{\Sigma_i,\Sigma_j\}=2\delta_{ij}
\end{equation*}

where
\begin{equation*}
\Sigma_i=\begin{pmatrix}\sigma_i&0\\0&\sigma_i\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

and $\sigma_i$ are the Pauli spin matrices.

\bigskip
Notes on the Eigenmath script:
\begin{enumerate}

\item
The $\Sigma_i$ matrices are formed using the \verb$kronecker$ function.

\item
Index $k$ is used instead of $i$ to avoid overriding imaginary unit $i$.

\item
The expression \verb$j == k$ is equivalent to $\delta_{jk}$.

\end{enumerate}
