For $x$ and $y$ we have
\begin{equation*}
x=\cos\theta,
\qquad
y=s\,\frac{d\sigma}{d\cos\theta}=2\pi s\,\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}
\end{equation*}

The cross section formula is
\begin{equation*}
\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}=\frac{(\alpha\hbar c)^2}{4s}\left(1+\cos^2\theta\right)
\end{equation*}

To compute predicted values $\hat{y}$, multiply by $10^{37}$ to convert square meters to nanobarns.
\begin{equation*}
\hat y=2\pi s\,\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}
=\frac{\pi(\alpha\hbar c)^2}{2}\left(1+x^2\right)\times10^{37}
\end{equation*}

The coefficient of determination is
\begin{equation*}
R^2=1-\frac{\sum(y-\hat{y})^2}{\sum(y-\bar{y})^2}=0.87
\end{equation*}

Hence $d\sigma$ explains 87\% of the variance in the data.
