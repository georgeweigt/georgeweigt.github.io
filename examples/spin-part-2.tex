\section*{Spin part 2}

Let $|s\rangle$ be a spin with polar angle $\theta$ and azimuth angle $\phi$.
\begin{equation*}
|s\rangle=\begin{pmatrix}\cos(\theta/2)\\[1ex]\sin(\theta/2)\,e^{i\phi}\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

Spin measurement probabilities are the transition probabilities from $|s\rangle$ to an eigenstate.

\bigskip
The eigenstates are given by the eigenvectors
\begin{align*}
|x_+\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\1\end{pmatrix}
&
|y_+\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\i\end{pmatrix}
&
|z_+\rangle&=\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}
\\[1ex]
|x_-\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\-1\end{pmatrix}
&
|y_-\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\-i\end{pmatrix}
&
|z_-\rangle&=\begin{pmatrix}0\\1\end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}

Hence in the $x$ direction
\begin{align*}
\Pr(+)
&=|\langle x_+|s\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}+\tfrac{1}{2}\sin\theta\cos\phi
\\[1ex]
\Pr(-)
&=|\langle x_-|s\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}-\tfrac{1}{2}\sin\theta\cos\phi
\end{align*}

In the $y$ direction
\begin{align*}
\Pr(+)
&=|\langle y_+|s\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}+\tfrac{1}{2}\sin\theta\sin\phi
\\[1ex]
\Pr(-)
&=|\langle y_-|s\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}-\tfrac{1}{2}\sin\theta\sin\phi
\end{align*}

In the $z$ direction
\begin{align*}
\Pr(+)
&=|\langle z_+|s\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}+\tfrac{1}{2}\cos\theta
\\[1ex]
\Pr(-)
&=|\langle z_-|s\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}-\tfrac{1}{2}\cos\theta
\end{align*}

For each direction we have $\Pr(+)+\Pr(-)=1$ as required by total probability.
