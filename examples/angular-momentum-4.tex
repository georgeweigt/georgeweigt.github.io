\section*{Angular momentum 4}

From the previous section we have quantum numbers $l$ and $m$ such that
\begin{equation*}
l=0,\tfrac{1}{2},1,\tfrac{3}{2},2,\ldots
\end{equation*}

and
\begin{equation*}
m=-l,-l+1,\ldots,l-1,l
\end{equation*}

The eigenvalues of $L^2$ are $l(l+1)\hbar$.
\begin{equation*}
L^2\psi=l(l+1)\hbar^2\psi
\end{equation*}

The eigenvalues of $L_z$ are $m\hbar$.
\begin{equation*}
L_z\psi=m\hbar\psi
\end{equation*}

We now seek eigenfunctions $\psi$ that solve
these eigenvalue equations.

\medskip
For integer $l$ the eigenfunctions are
spherical harmonics $Y_{lm}$.
\begin{equation*}
\psi=Y_{lm}(\theta,\phi)
\end{equation*}

Hence
\begin{equation*}
L^2Y_{lm}=l(l+1)\hbar^2Y_{lm}
\end{equation*}

and
\begin{equation*}
L_zY_{lm}=m\hbar Y_{lm}
\end{equation*}

It turns out that $L^2$ and $L_z$ have no eigenfunctions with half-integer eigenvalues.
