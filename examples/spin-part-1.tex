\section*{Spin part 1}

Spin matrices are given by
\begin{equation*}
S_x=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}
\qquad
S_y=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{pmatrix}0&-i\\i&\phantom{-}0\end{pmatrix}
\qquad
S_z=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{pmatrix}1&\phantom{-}0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

Given the eigenvectors
\begin{align*}
|x_+\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\1\end{pmatrix}
&
|y_+\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\i\end{pmatrix}
&
|z_+\rangle&=\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}
\\[1ex]
|x_-\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\-1\end{pmatrix}
&
|y_-\rangle&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\-i\end{pmatrix}
&
|z_-\rangle&=\begin{pmatrix}0\\1\end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}

spin matrices can also be written as
\begin{align*}
S_x&=\frac{\hbar}{2}\,|x_+\rangle\langle x_+| - \frac{\hbar}{2}\,|x_-\rangle\langle x_-|
\\[1ex]
S_y&=\frac{\hbar}{2}\,|y_+\rangle\langle y_+| - \frac{\hbar}{2}\,|y_-\rangle\langle y_-|
\\[1ex]
S_z&=\frac{\hbar}{2}\,|z_+\rangle\langle z_+| - \frac{\hbar}{2}\,|z_-\rangle\langle z_-|
\end{align*}

where $\pm\hbar/2$ are eigenvalues.
