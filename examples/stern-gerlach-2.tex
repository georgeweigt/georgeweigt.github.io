\section*{Stern-Gerlach 2}

From the previous section we have the Schr\"odinger equations
\begin{align*}
-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_1+\mu_B(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_1
=i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_1
\\[1ex]
-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_2-\mu_B(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_2
=i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_2
\end{align*}

We now seek eigenfunctions for $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$.

\medskip
The paper ``Construction of Exact Solutions for the Stern-Gerlach Effect''
provides the following solutions.
\begin{align*}
\psi_1&=\operatorname{Ai}\left[
\left(\frac{\mu_B\alpha m}{\hbar^2}\right)^{1/3}
\left(\frac{\mu_B\alpha}{4m}\right)t^2
+\left(\frac{\mu_B\alpha m}{\hbar^2}\right)^{1/3}z\right]
\exp\left(-\frac{i\alpha\mu_Bzt}{2\hbar}\right)
\exp\left(-\frac{\mu_BB_0t}{\hbar}\right)
\\[1ex]
\psi_2&=\operatorname{Ai}\left[
\left(\frac{\mu_B\alpha m}{\hbar^2}\right)^{1/3}
\left(\frac{\mu_B\alpha}{4m}\right)t^2
-\left(\frac{\mu_B\alpha m}{\hbar^2}\right)^{1/3}z\right]
\exp\left(+\frac{i\alpha\mu_Bzt}{2\hbar}\right)
\exp\left(+\frac{\mu_BB_0t}{\hbar}\right)
\end{align*}

Let us construct polynomial approximations for $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$.

\medskip
For the Airy function we have
\begin{equation*}
\operatorname{Ai}(x)\approx\sum_{k=0}^n a_kx^k
\end{equation*}

where
\begin{align*}
a_0&=\frac{1}{3^{2/3}\Gamma(2/3)}
\\
a_1&=-\frac{1}{3^{1/3}\Gamma(1/3)}
\\
a_2&=0
\\\
a_{n+3}&=\frac{a_n}{(n+3)(n+2)}
\end{align*}

For $n=9$ we obtain
\begin{multline*}
\operatorname{Ai}(x)\approx
2.73941\times10^{-5}x^9
-0.000513531x^7
+0.00197238x^6
\\
-0.0215683x^4
+0.0591713x^3
-0.258819x
+0.355028
\end{multline*}

Now calculate $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ using the $\operatorname{Ai}(x)$ approximation.

\medskip
Calculate departures from equality of the Schr\"odinger equations for $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$.
\begin{align*}
\epsilon_1&=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_1
+\mu_B(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_1
-i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_1
\\[1ex]
\epsilon_2&=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_2
-\mu_B(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_2
-i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_2
\end{align*}

% epsilon1
% = 2.1684 10^(-19) alpha^(16/3) muB^(16/3) t^6 z^2 / (hbar^(8/3) m^(5/3))
% + 2.1684 10^(-19) alpha^5 muB^5 t^6 z / (hbar^2 m^2)
% + 8.67362 10^(-19) alpha^(13/3) muB^(13/3) t^4 z^3 / (hbar^(8/3) m^(2/3))
% + 1.73472 10^(-18) alpha^4 muB^4 t^4 z^2 / (hbar^2 m)
% - 1.73472 10^(-18) alpha^(10/3) muB^(10/3) t^4 / (hbar^(2/3) m^(5/3))
% - 1.38778 10^(-17) alpha^(7/3) muB^(7/3) t^2 z / (hbar^(2/3) m^(2/3))
% - 2.77556 10^(-17) alpha^(4/3) m^(1/3) muB^(4/3) z^2 / hbar^(2/3)
% + 8.67362 10^(-19) i alpha^4 muB^4 t^3 z^4 / hbar^3

After calculating $\epsilon_1$ and $\epsilon_2$, cancel exponentials and
zero out factors $t^n$ and $z^m$ where $n\ge8$ and $m\ge5$.

\medskip
The results are
\begin{multline*}
\epsilon_1=
\boxed{2.1684\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{16/3} \mu_B^{16/3} t^6 z^2}{\hbar^{8/3} m^{5/3}}
+\boxed{2.1684\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^5 \mu_B^5 t^6 z}{\hbar^2 m^2}
\\
+\boxed{8.67362\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{13/3} \mu_B^{13/3} t^4 z^3}{\hbar^{8/3} m^{2/3}}
+\boxed{1.73472\times10^{-18}}\,\frac{ \alpha^4 \mu_B^4 t^4 z^2}{\hbar^2 m}
\\
-\boxed{1.73472\times10^{-18}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{10/3} \mu_B^{10/3} t^4}{\hbar^{2/3} m^{5/3}}
-\boxed{1.38778\times10^{-17}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{7/3} \mu_B^{7/3} t^2 z}{\hbar^{2/3} m^{2/3}}
\\
-\boxed{2.77556\times10^{-17}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{4/3} m^{1/3} \mu_B^{4/3} z^2}{\hbar^{2/3}}
+\boxed{8.67362\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ i \alpha^4 \mu_B^4 t^3 z^4}{\hbar^3}
\end{multline*}

% epsilon2
% = 2.1684 10^(-19) alpha^(16/3) muB^(16/3) t^6 z^2 / (hbar^(8/3) m^(5/3))
% - 2.1684 10^(-19) alpha^5 muB^5 t^6 z / (hbar^2 m^2)
% - 8.67362 10^(-19) alpha^(13/3) muB^(13/3) t^4 z^3 / (hbar^(8/3) m^(2/3))
% + 1.73472 10^(-18) alpha^4 muB^4 t^4 z^2 / (hbar^2 m)
% - 1.73472 10^(-18) alpha^(10/3) muB^(10/3) t^4 / (hbar^(2/3) m^(5/3))
% + 1.38778 10^(-17) alpha^(7/3) muB^(7/3) t^2 z / (hbar^(2/3) m^(2/3))
% - 2.77556 10^(-17) alpha^(4/3) m^(1/3) muB^(4/3) z^2 / hbar^(2/3)
% + 8.67362 10^(-19) i alpha^4 muB^4 t^3 z^4 / hbar^3

and
\begin{multline*}
\epsilon_2
=\boxed{2.1684\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{16/3} \mu_B^{16/3} t^6 z^2}{\hbar^{8/3} m^{5/3}}
-\boxed{2.1684\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^5 \mu_B^5 t^6 z}{\hbar^2 m^2}
\\
-\boxed{8.67362\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{13/3} \mu_B^{13/3} t^4 z^3}{\hbar^{8/3} m^{2/3}}
+\boxed{1.73472\times10^{-18}}\,\frac{ \alpha^4 \mu_B^4 t^4 z^2}{\hbar^2 m}
\\
-\boxed{1.73472\times10^{-18}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{10/3} \mu_B^{10/3} t^4}{\hbar^{2/3} m^{5/3}}
+\boxed{1.38778\times10^{-17}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{7/3} \mu_B^{7/3} t^2 z}{\hbar^{2/3} m^{2/3}}
\\
-\boxed{2.77556\times10^{-17}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{4/3} m^{1/3} \mu_B^{4/3} z^2}{\hbar^{2/3}}
+\boxed{8.67362\times10^{-19}}\,\frac{ i \alpha^4 \mu_B^4 t^3 z^4}{\hbar^3}
\end{multline*}

All of the numerical values are small relative to the coefficients in
$\operatorname{Ai}(x)$ hence $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ are confirmed as solutions.
