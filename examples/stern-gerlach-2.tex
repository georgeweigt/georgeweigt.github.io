\section*{Stern-Gerlach 2}

From the previous section we have the following Schr\"odinger equations
for a Stern-Gerlach experiment.
\begin{align*}
-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_1+\frac{e\hbar}{2m}(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_1
=i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_1
\\[1ex]
-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_2-\frac{e\hbar}{2m}(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_2
=i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_2
\end{align*}

We now seek solutions for $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$.

\medskip
An online paper\footnote{``Construction of Exact Solutions for the Stern-Gerlach Effect'' by Bulnes and Oliveira.}
provides the following solutions.
\begin{align*}
\psi_1&=\operatorname{Ai}\left[
\left(\frac{\alpha e}{2\hbar}\right)^{1/3}
\left(\frac{\alpha e\hbar}{8m^2}\right)t^2
+\left(\frac{\alpha e}{2\hbar}\right)^{1/3}z\right]
\exp\left(-\frac{i\alpha ezt}{4m}\right)
\exp\left(-\frac{iB_0et}{2m}\right)
\\[1ex]
\psi_2&=\operatorname{Ai}\left[
\left(\frac{\alpha e}{2\hbar}\right)^{1/3}
\left(\frac{\alpha e\hbar}{8m^2}\right)t^2
-\left(\frac{\alpha e}{2\hbar}\right)^{1/3}z\right]
\exp\left(+\frac{i\alpha ezt}{4m}\right)
\exp\left(+\frac{iB_0et}{2m}\right)
\end{align*}

Let us try verifying $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ with a Taylor series
expansion of the Airy function $\operatorname{Ai}(x)$.

\medskip
We have
\begin{equation*}
\operatorname{Ai}(x)\approx\sum_{k=0}^n a_kx^k
\end{equation*}

where
\begin{align*}
a_0&=\frac{1}{3^{2/3}\Gamma(2/3)}
\\
a_1&=-\frac{1}{3^{1/3}\Gamma(1/3)}
\\
a_2&=0
\\\
a_{n+3}&=\frac{a_n}{(n+3)(n+2)}
\end{align*}

For $n=9$ we obtain
\begin{multline*}
\operatorname{Ai}(x)\approx
2.73941\times10^{-5}x^9
-0.000513531x^7
+0.00197238x^6
\\
-0.0215683x^4
+0.0591713x^3
-0.258819x
+0.355028
\end{multline*}

Calculate $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ using the $\operatorname{Ai}(x)$ approximation.

\medskip
To verify $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$,
calculate departures from equality of the Schr\"odinger equations.
\begin{align*}
\epsilon_1&=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_1
+\mu_B(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_1
-i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_1
\\[1ex]
\epsilon_2&=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}\psi_2
-\mu_B(B_0+\alpha z)\psi_2
-i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi_2
\end{align*}

After calculating $\epsilon_1$ and $\epsilon_2$, cancel exponentials and
zero out factors $t^n$ and $z^m$ where $n\ge8$ and $m\ge5$.

\medskip
The results are
\begin{multline*}
\epsilon_1
 = -1.69407\times\boxed{10^{-20}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{16/3} e^{16/3} \hbar^{8/3} t^6 z^2}{m^7}
 + 1.69407\times\boxed{10^{-21}}\,\frac{ \alpha^5 e^5 \hbar^3 t^6 z}{m^7}
 \\
 - 1.35525\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{13/3} e^{13/3} \hbar^{5/3} t^4 z^3}{m^5}
 - 4.33681\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{10/3} e^{10/3} \hbar^{2/3} t^2 z^4}{m^3}
 \\
 - 3.46945\times\boxed{10^{-18}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{7/3} e^{7/3} \hbar^{5/3} t^2 z}{m^3}
 - 1.38778\times\boxed{10^{-17}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{4/3} e^{4/3} \hbar^{2/3} z^2}{m}
 \\
 - 1.0842\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ i \alpha^{13/3} e^{13/3} \hbar^{8/3} t^5 z}{m^6}
 + 8.67362\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ i \alpha^{10/3} e^{10/3} \hbar^{5/3} t^3 z^2}{m^4}
\end{multline*}

and
\begin{multline*}
\epsilon_2
 = -1.69407\times\boxed{10^{-20}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{16/3} e^{16/3} \hbar^{8/3} t^6 z^2}{m^7}
 - 1.69407\times\boxed{10^{-21}}\,\frac{ \alpha^5 e^5 \hbar^3 t^6 z}{m^7}
 \\
 + 1.35525\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{13/3} e^{13/3} \hbar^{5/3} t^4 z^3}{m^5}
 - 4.33681\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{10/3} e^{10/3} \hbar^{2/3} t^2 z^4}{m^3}
 \\
 + 3.46945\times\boxed{10^{-18}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{7/3} e^{7/3} \hbar^{5/3} t^2 z}{m^3}
 - 1.38778\times\boxed{10^{-17}}\,\frac{ \alpha^{4/3} e^{4/3} \hbar^{2/3} z^2}{m}
 \\
 + 1.0842\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ i \alpha^{13/3} e^{13/3} \hbar^{8/3} t^5 z}{m^6}
 + 8.67362\times\boxed{10^{-19}}\,\frac{ i \alpha^{10/3} e^{10/3} \hbar^{5/3} t^3 z^2}{m^4}
\end{multline*}

The numerical values are round off errors
hence $\epsilon_1=\epsilon_2=0$ and $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ are confirmed as solutions.

\subsubsection*{Notes}

\begin{enumerate}

\item
Wavefunctions $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ given above are dimensionless.
Recall that
\begin{equation*}
\int\psi^*\psi\,dx=1
\end{equation*}
%
Hence, in order to be dimensionally correct,
$\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ must be multiplied by a unit factor of
inverse square root of length to cancel with $dx$.

\end{enumerate}
