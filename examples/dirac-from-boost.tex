\section*{Dirac from boost}

Let $u_0$ be the spinor for an electron at rest with spin up along the $z$ axis.
\begin{equation*}
u_0=\begin{pmatrix}\sqrt{2m}\\0\\0\\0\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

Matrix $A$ boosts $u_0$ in the $z$ direction where $E=\sqrt{p_z^2+m^2}$.
\begin{equation*}
A=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2m(E+m)}}
\begin{pmatrix}
E+m & 0 & -p_z & 0\\
0 & E+m & 0 & p_z\\
p_z & 0 & E+m & 0\\
0 & -p_z & 0 & E+m\\
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

Hence
\begin{align*}
u&=Au_0
\\
&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2m(E+m)}}
\begin{pmatrix}
E+m & 0 & -p_z & 0\\
0 & E+m & 0 & p_z\\
p_z & 0 & E+m & 0\\
0 & -p_z & 0 & E+m\\
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}\sqrt{2m}\\0\\0\\0\end{pmatrix}
\\
&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{E+m}}\begin{pmatrix}E+m\\0\\p_z\\0\end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}

Noting that $E=m$ in the rest frame, spinor $u$ has the same normalization as $u_0$.
\begin{equation*}
u^\dag u=2E
\end{equation*}

Recall the Dirac equation for spinors.
\begin{equation*}
\slashed pu=mu
\end{equation*}

Substitute $Au_0$ for $u$ on the right-hand side.
\begin{equation*}
\slashed pu=mAu_0
\end{equation*}

By the identity $\gamma^0u_0=u_0$, substitute $\gamma^0u_0$ for $u_0$.
\begin{equation*}
\slashed pu=mA\gamma^0u_0
\end{equation*}

Substitute $A^{-1}u$ for $u_0$.
\begin{equation*}
\slashed pu=mA\gamma^0A^{-1}u
\end{equation*}

Cancel $u$ to obtain
\begin{equation*}
\slashed p=mA\gamma^0A^{-1}
\end{equation*}

Divide both sides by $m$.
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\slashed p}{m}=A\gamma^0A^{-1}
\end{equation*}

Right multiply both sides by $A$.
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\slashed p}{m}A=A\gamma^0
\tag{1}
\end{equation*}

To recover the Dirac equation from boost $A$, start with the identity
\begin{equation*}
\gamma^0u_0=u_0
\end{equation*}

Boost both sides of the equation.
\begin{equation*}
A\gamma^0u_0=Au_0
\end{equation*}

By equation (1) substitute $(\slashed p/m)A$ for $A\gamma^0$.
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\slashed p}{m}Au_0=Au_0
\end{equation*}

Substitute $u$ for $Au_0$.
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\slashed p}{m}u=u
\end{equation*}

Multiply both sides by $m$ to obtain the Dirac equation.
\begin{equation*}
\slashed pu=mu
\tag{2}
\end{equation*}
