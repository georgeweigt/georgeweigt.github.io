\section*{Harmonic oscillator coherent state}

A coherent state minimizes uncertainty.
The ground state is a coherent state.
To make the ground state more interesting, parameters $r$ and $\theta$ are added
to shift $\langle x\rangle$ and $\langle p\rangle$ from zero.
%
\begin{multline*}
\psi_{n,r,\theta}(x,t)
=\frac{(-1)^n}{\sqrt{2^nn!}}
\left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi\hbar}\right)^\frac{1}{4}
\left(\frac{\hbar}{m\omega}\right)^{n/2}
\\
{}\times
\exp\left[
\frac{m\omega}{2\hbar}\left(x-\langle x\rangle\right)^2
+\frac{i}{\hbar}\langle p\rangle\left(x-\tfrac{1}{2}\langle x\rangle\right)
-i\left(n+\tfrac{1}{2}\right)\omega t
\right]
\frac{d^n}{dx^n}\exp\left[-\frac{m\omega}{\hbar}\left(x-\langle x\rangle\right)^2\right]
\end{multline*}

Parameters $r$ and $\theta$ are polar coordinates in phase space such that
\begin{equation*}
\langle x\rangle=\sqrt{\frac{2\hbar}{m\omega}}\,r\cos(\omega t+\theta)
\end{equation*}

and
\begin{equation*}
\langle p\rangle=-\sqrt{2m\hbar\omega}\,r\sin(\omega t+\theta)
\end{equation*}

Verify $\psi_{n,r,\theta}(x,t)$ solves the Schrodinger equation
\begin{equation*}
i\hbar\frac{d}{dt}\psi(x,t)=\hat H\psi(x,t)
\end{equation*}

where
\begin{equation*}
\hat H=\frac{\hat p^2}{2m}+\frac{m\omega^2x^2}{2}
\end{equation*}

For the ground state $\psi_{0,r,\theta}(x,t)$ verify that
\begin{equation*}
\Delta x=\sqrt{\langle x^2\rangle-\langle x\rangle^2}
=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2m\omega}}
\end{equation*}

and
\begin{equation*}
\Delta p=\sqrt{\langle p^2\rangle-\langle p\rangle^2}
=\sqrt{\frac{m\hbar\omega}{2}}
\end{equation*}

Hence $\Delta x\Delta p$ is the minimum allowed by the uncertainty principle.
\begin{equation*}
\Delta x\Delta p=\frac{\hbar}{2}
\end{equation*}
