\section*{Alpha decay 2}

Let our hypothesis be that the nucleus produces alpha particles at an average rate $\nu$
which then tunnel with probability $T$.
Hence the decay rate $\lambda$ is
\begin{equation*}
\lambda=\nu T
\end{equation*}

The half-life $\tau_{1/2}$ is
\begin{equation*}
\tau_{1/2}=\frac{\log2}{\lambda}=\frac{\log2}{\nu T}
\end{equation*}

We seek to calculate $\tau_{1/2}$ from $T$.
The problem is that $\nu$ is only known empirically so we
use the Geiger-Nuttall formula
\begin{equation*}
\log_{10}\tau_{1/2}=-C_1-C_2Z^{2/3}+C_3\frac{Z}{\sqrt{E}}
\tag{1}
\end{equation*}

where
\begin{align*}
C_1&=28.9
\\
C_2&=1.60
\\
C_3&=1.61
\end{align*}

For the alpha decay process
${}^{238}_{\phantom{0}92}\mathrm U\rightarrow{}^{234}_{\phantom{0}90}\mathrm{Th}$
we have
\begin{align*}
Z&=90
\\
E&=4.27\,\text{MeV}
\end{align*}

Hence
\begin{equation*}
\tau_{1/2}=1.2\times10^9\,\text{yr}
\end{equation*}

The actual half-life of ${}^{238}\mathrm U$ is
\begin{equation*}
4.463\times10^9\,\text{yr}
\end{equation*}
