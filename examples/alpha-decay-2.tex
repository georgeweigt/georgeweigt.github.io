\section*{Alpha decay 2}

Let our hypothesis be that the nucleus produces alpha particles at an average rate $\nu$
which then tunnel with probability $e^{-2\gamma}$.
Hence the decay rate $\lambda$ is
\begin{equation*}
\lambda=\nu e^{-2\gamma}
\end{equation*}

The half-life $\tau_{1/2}$ is
\begin{equation*}
\tau_{1/2}=\frac{\log2}{\lambda}=\frac{\log2}{\nu}e^{2\gamma}
\end{equation*}

We seek to calculate $\tau_{1/2}$ from $\gamma$.
The problem is that $\nu$ is only known empirically so we
use the following modified Geiger-Nuttall formula
\begin{equation*}
\log\tau_{1/2}=3.682\frac{Z}{\sqrt{E}}-120.8
\end{equation*}

where $\tau_{1/2}$ is time in seconds and $E$ is energy in MeV.

\bigskip
For the alpha decay process
${}^{238}_{\phantom{0}92}\mathrm U\rightarrow{}^{234}_{\phantom{0}90}\mathrm{Th}$
we have
\begin{align*}
Z&=90
\\
E&=4.27\,\text{MeV}
\end{align*}

Hence
\begin{equation*}
\tau_{1/2}=1.49\times10^{17}\,\text{s}=4.72\times10^9\,\text{yr}
\end{equation*}

The actual half-life of ${}^{238}\mathrm U$ is
\begin{equation*}
4.46\times10^9\,\text{yr}
\end{equation*}
