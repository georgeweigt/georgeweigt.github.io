\section*{Geiger and Marsden scattering data}

Data from Geiger and Marsden's 1913 paper
``The Laws of Deflexion of $\alpha$ Particles through Large Angles.''
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
\theta & \text{Silver} & \text{Gold}
\\[1ex]
150 & 22.2 & 33.1\\
135 & 27.4 & 43.0\\
120 & 33.0 & 51.9\\
105 & 47.3 & 69.5\\
75 & 136 & 211\\\
60 & 320 & 477\\
45 & 989 & 1435\\
37.5 & 1760 & 3300\\
30 & 5260 & 7800\\
22.5 & 20300 & 27300\\
15 & 105400 & 132000
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

The predicted values are
\begin{equation*}
\hat y=\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}=\frac{\beta}{(1-\cos\theta)^2}
\end{equation*}

By fitting $\beta$ values using ordinary least squares we obtain
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
& \text{Silver} & \text{Gold}
\\[1ex]
\beta & 122 & 153\\
R^2 & 0.999 & 0.999
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

where $R^2$ is the coefficient of determination
\begin{equation*}
R^2=1-\frac{\sum(y-\hat y)^2}{(y-\bar y)^2}
\end{equation*}
