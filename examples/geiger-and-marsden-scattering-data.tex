\section*{Geiger and Marsden scattering data}

Data from Geiger and Marsden's 1913 paper
``The Laws of Deflexion of $\alpha$ Particles through Large Angles.''
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
\theta & \text{Silver} & \text{Gold}
\\[1ex]
150 & 22.2 & 33.1\\
135 & 27.4 & 43.0\\
120 & 33.0 & 51.9\\
105 & 47.3 & 69.5\\
75 & 136 & 211\\\
60 & 320 & 477\\
45 & 989 & 1435\\
37.5 & 1760 & 3300\\
30 & 5260 & 7800\\
22.5 & 20300 & 27300\\
15 & 105400 & 132000
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

The authors model the data as proportional to $1/\sin^4(\theta/2)$ which is equivalent to
\begin{equation*}
\hat y=\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}=\frac{\beta}{(1-\cos\theta)^2}
\end{equation*}

Using ordinary least squares we obtain
$\beta=122.0$ for silver and $\beta=153.4$ for gold.
The resulting predicted values are
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
\theta & \text{Silver} & \text{Gold}
\\[1ex]
150 & 35.0 & 44.0\\
135 & 41.9 & 52.6\\
120 & 54.2 & 68.2\\
105 & 77 & 96.8\\
75 & 222 & 279\\\
60 & 488 & 614\\
45 & 1423 & 1788\\
37.5 & 2858 & 3592\\
30 & 6799 & 8545\\
22.5 & 21062 & 26471\\
15 & 105111 & 132104
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

The coefficient of determination $R^2$ is given by
\begin{equation*}
R^2=1-\frac{\sum(y-\hat y)^2}{\sum(y-\bar y)^2}
\end{equation*}

$R^2$ for both gold and silver is greater than 0.999 indicating
that 99.9\% of the variance in the data is explained by $\hat y$.
