\section*{Born approximation 3}

We will now show why we can use the approximation
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\exp(ik|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|)}{|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|}\,V(\mathbf y)
\approx\frac{1}{|\mathbf x|}
\exp\left(ik|\mathbf x|-\frac{ik\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}\right)V(\mathbf y)
\end{equation*}

For scattering experiments we have $\mathbf x$ far from the origin where $V(\mathbf x)\approx0$.
Hence we can restrict our attention to the $\mathbf y$-ball such that
$|\mathbf y|\ll|\mathbf x|$ and let $V(\mathbf y)$ censor everything else.

\medskip
Consider the following expansions.
\begin{align*}
|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|^2
&=|\mathbf x|^2-2\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y+|\mathbf y|^2
\\[1ex]
\left(|\mathbf x|-\frac{\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}\right)^2
&=|\mathbf x|^2-2\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y
+\left(\frac{\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}\right)^2
\end{align*}

For $|\mathbf x|^2\gg|\mathbf y|^2$ we have
\begin{equation*}
|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|^2
\approx
\left(|\mathbf x|-\frac{\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}\right)^2
\end{equation*}

Noting that $|\mathbf x|^2>\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y$ we have by positivity
\begin{equation*}
|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|\approx|\mathbf x|-\frac{\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}
\end{equation*}

Hence
\begin{equation*}
\exp(ik|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|)
\approx
\exp\left(ik|\mathbf x|-\frac{ik\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}\right)
\tag{1}
\end{equation*}

For the factor
\begin{equation*}
\frac{1}{|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|}
\end{equation*}

we discard $\mathbf y$ altogether and use
\begin{equation*}
\frac{1}{|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|}\approx\frac{1}{|\mathbf x|}
\tag{2}
\end{equation*}

Hence by (1) and (2) we have
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\exp(ik|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|)}{|\mathbf x-\mathbf y|}\,V(\mathbf y)
\approx\frac{1}{|\mathbf x|}
\exp\left(ik|\mathbf x|-\frac{ik\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y}{|\mathbf x|}\right)V(\mathbf y)
\end{equation*}
