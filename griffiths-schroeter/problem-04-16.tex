4.16
What is the {\it most probable} value of $r$, in the ground state of hydrogen?
(The answer is {\it not} zero!)
{\it Hint:} First you must figure out the probability that the electron
would be found between $r$ and $r+dr$.
}

Function $f(r)$ is the radial probability density function for the electron.
\begin{align*}
f(r)&=\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^\pi|\psi_{100}|^2r^2\sin\theta\,d\theta\,d\phi
\\
&=4\pi|\psi_{100}|^2r^2
\\
&=\frac{4r^2}{a_0^3}\exp\left(-\frac{2r}{a_0}\right)\tag{1}
\end{align*}

The derivative of $f(r)$.
\begin{equation*}
\frac{df}{dr}=\frac{8r}{a_0^3}\left(1-\frac{r}{a_0}\right)\exp\left(-\frac{2r}{a_0}\right)\tag{2}
\end{equation*}

Solve for $df/dr=0$.
\begin{equation*}
r=a_0
\end{equation*}
