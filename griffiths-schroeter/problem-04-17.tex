4.17
Calculate $\langle z\hat Hz\rangle$, in the ground state of hydrogen.
{\it Hint:} This takes two pages and six integrals, or four lines and no
integrals, depending on how you set it up.
To do it the quick way, start by noting that
$[z,[H,z]]=2zHz-Hz^2-z^2H$.
}

Noting that
\begin{equation*}
[z,[H,z]]\psi=z([H,z]\psi)-([H,z]\psi)z=0
\end{equation*}

we have
\begin{equation*}
zHz=\frac{1}{2}(Hz^2+z^2H)
\end{equation*}

Hence
\begin{align*}
\langle z\hat Hz\rangle&=\frac{1}{2}\langle\psi_{100}|(\hat Hz^2+z^2\hat H)|\psi_{100}\rangle
\\
&=\frac{1}{2}\langle\psi_{100}|(E_1z^2+z^2E_1)|\psi_{100}\rangle
\\
&=E_1\langle z^2\rangle
\end{align*}

From problem 4.15
\begin{equation*}
E_1\langle z^2\rangle=E_1a_0^2\tag{1}
\end{equation*}
